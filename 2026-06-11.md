## Breakthroughs & Research

### CVPR 2026 — Award-Winning Papers (June 9)
The **Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR 2026)** announced award-winning papers from **4,089 accepted papers out of 16,092 submissions**. A standout paper from Google DeepMind and university collaborators introduced **D4RT**, a network that reconstructs the geometry and motion of dynamic 4D scenes directly from video.
- Source: [Newswise via Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)

### Google TurboQuant — KV Cache Compression (ICLR 2026)
Google unveiled **TurboQuant** at ICLR 2026, an algorithm that reduces KV cache memory overhead using PolarQuant vector rotation and Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss compression — enabling massive-context-window models to run far more efficiently.
- Source: [Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)

### AI Outperforms Physicians in Diagnostics
A study published in **Science** (Harvard Medical School / Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center) found that an **OpenAI reasoning model outperformed experienced physicians** at diagnosing patients using only electronic health records.
- Source: [Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)

### Climate AI — 25× Faster Projections
A UC San Diego study showed that **combining generative AI with physics-based data produces climate projections ~25 times faster** than traditional simulation methods.
- Source: [Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)

### Memory Tools Can Degrade AI Models (June 10)
New research published and covered by TechCrunch explores how memory mechanisms can paradoxically **reduce model performance**, raising questions about design tradeoffs in long-context and persistent-memory systems.
- Source: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/10/how-memory-tools-can-make-ai-models-worse/)

## Regulatory & Policy

### Prompt Injection Attacks Classified as CVE (May 2026)
**Prompt injection attacks on AI systems are now officially classified as a CVE vulnerability category.** CVEs linked to AI-generated code increased **sixfold** compared to 2025.
- Source: [AIApps](https://www.aiapps.com/blog/ai-news-breakthroughs-launches-trends-must-read/)

### EU AI Act — Enforcement Timeline Update
- Most **EU AI Act** provisions begin enforcement on **August 2, 2026**
- Synthetic media transparency requirements accelerated to **December 2, 2026**
- C2PA watermarking standards will be mandatory for AI-generated content in Europe
- High-risk system audits postponed to **December 2027** (per the Omnibus revision, May 7)
- Source: [AIApps](https://www.aiapps.com/blog/ai-news-breakthroughs-launches-trends-must-read/)

### Pennsylvania Sues Character.AI (June 8)
