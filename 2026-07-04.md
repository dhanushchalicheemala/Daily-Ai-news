## Startups & Funding

- **Venice AI becomes a unicorn with $65M Series A** — The privacy-first AI platform, founded by crypto veteran Erik Voorhees, raised its first outside capital in a round led by Dragonfly (with Coinbase Ventures, F-Prime, and North Island Ventures participating), valuing the company at $1B. ## IPOs & Corporate Moves

- **AWS launches $1B forward-deployed engineering unit** — Amazon is investing $1 billion of its own balance sheet (no outside investors) to create a Forward Deployed Engineering organization, embedding five-to-six-person engineer pods inside customer companies for 45-day sprints to deploy custom AI agents. Early customers include the Allen Institute, Cox Automotive, the NBA, the NFL, Ricoh, and Southwest Airlines. The move follows similar forward-deployed pushes from OpenAI (~$4B venture) and Anthropic (~$1.5B venture). ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/amazon-launches-new-1-billion-fde-org-following-openai-and-anthropic/))

- **California signs discounted Claude deal for state government** — Governor Gavin Newsom announced a first-of-its-kind partnership giving all California state agencies, cities, and counties access to Claude at a 50% discount via the state's new Statewide IT Shared Services portal, plus free workforce training from Anthropic. The DMV and Department of Health Care Services are already piloting Claude internally. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/29/anthropic-and-gov-newsom-forge-deal-allowing-california-government-to-use-claude-at-half-price/), [Governor of California](https://www.gov.ca.gov/2026/06/29/governor-newsom-announces-a-first-of-its-kind-partnership-providing-anthropic-tools-to-state-agencies-and-improving-services-for-californians/))

## New Models & Tools

- **Qualcomm unveils HBC memory architecture and AI250/AI300 data-center chips** — At its Investor Day, Qualcomm revealed "High Bandwidth Compute" (HBC), a near-memory 3D-stacked accelerator design that ditches expensive HBM for mobile-oriented LPDDR stacks, claiming 6x bandwidth-per-watt over HBM and 200x capacity-per-watt over on-chip SRAM. The AI250 accelerator hits 133 TB/s single-card memory bandwidth; Microsoft and Meta are named as early customers of the Dragonfly data-center portfolio. ([Nikkei Asia](https://asia.nikkei.com/business/technology/artificial-intelligence/qualcomm-challenges-nvidia-s-ai-grip-with-chip-that-ditches-hbm), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/qualcomm-reveals-hbc-near-memory-ai-architecture-ai250-and-ai350-accelerators-touts-6x-higher-bandwidth-per-watt-compared-to-hbm-200x-capacity-compared-to-on-chip-sram))

- **Claude Sonnet 5 rollout continues as free/Pro default** — Anthropic's newest Sonnet, released June 30, remains the default model for all Free and Pro users on claude.ai, offering performance approaching Opus 4.8 at introductory pricing of $2/$10 per 1M tokens (through August 31) with a 1M-token context window. ([llm-stats.com](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates))
