## Breakthroughs & Research

- **OpenAI model disproves 80-year-old Erdős conjecture** — An internal OpenAI model generated the key ideas disproving the Erdős unit distance conjecture in discrete geometry. Mathematicians including Fields Medalist Tim Gowers verified and endorsed the result. In a parallel effort, Google DeepMind's AlphaProof Nexus solved nine additional Erdős problems using formal Lean proofs. ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/model-disproves-discrete-geometry-conjecture/) / [Memeburn](https://memeburn.com/ai-math-breakthrough-openai-disproves-80-year-erdos-conjecture/))

- **Photonic AI platform delivers expert-level medical diagnostics at 246× GPU efficiency** — Researchers at Shenzhen University developed an all-fiber photonic AI system using black phosphorus-based modulators that achieves expert-level accuracy for retinal detachment and liver cancer diagnosis while consuming a fraction of conventional GPU power. ([Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates))

- **Hybrid AI cuts climate model compute by 25×** — Combining generative AI with physics-based modelling reduces the time required to run high-resolution climate simulations by roughly an order of magnitude, with researchers noting accuracy is preserved across extreme-weather scenarios. ([Build This Now](https://www.buildthisnow.com/blog/guide/mechanics/ai-research-june-2026))

- **Inference costs down 100× year-on-year** — Independent benchmarks show per-token inference costs have dropped up to 100× compared to mid-2025, driven by hardware improvements, model distillation, and competitive pricing pressure from providers including Baseten, Together AI, and Groq. ([devFlokers](https://www.devflokers.com/blog/ai-tech-news-model-releases-june-2026))
