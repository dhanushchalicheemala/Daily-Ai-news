## New Models & Tools

- **Claude Fable 5 & Mythos 5 (Anthropic)** — Released June 9 with 95% on SWE-bench Verified, Fable 5 was immediately considered the top publicly accessible coding model. However, on **June 12, the US government ordered Anthropic to suspend both models** within 90 minutes after a jailbreak vulnerability (published by "Pliny the Liberator" on June 10) was flagged by Amazon CEO Andy Jassy to the White House. As of June 17, Anthropic and White House officials remain at an impasse with **no restoration timeline announced**. ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/09/anthropic-released-claude-fable-5-its-most-powerful-model-publicly-days-after-warning-ai-is-getting-too-dangerous/) / [unrot.co](https://unrot.co/blogs/ai-news-today-june-17-2026))

- **GLM-5.2 (Zhipu AI)** — Released June 13 and **open-sourced under MIT license on June 16**, directly framed as a response to the Fable 5 suspension. GLM-5.2 is currently topping BridgeBench Reasoning benchmarks and described as the most powerful text-only open-weights LLM available. ([AI Coding Daily](https://aicodingdaily.substack.com/p/kimi-k27-code-fable-5-ban-and-cursor) / [LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news))

- **Kimi K2.7 Code (Moonshot AI)** — Released June 12 with open weights under a Modified MIT license. Ranks near Fable 5 on coding benchmarks at a fraction of the cost; developer consensus is using it as the go-to coding model while Fable 5 remains offline. ([Lushbinary](https://lushbinary.com/blog/kimi-k2-7-code-vs-claude-fable-5-gpt-5-5-coding-comparison/))

- **GPT-5.5 (OpenAI)** — Achieved **82.7% on Terminal-Bench 2.0** and 52.5% fewer hallucinations than its predecessor. OpenAI also introduced **Deployment Simulation** (June 16), which replays past conversations through candidate models before release. ([LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news))

- **Gemini 3.1 Ultra & 3.5 Flash (Google)** — Gemini 3.1 Ultra features a **2-million-token context window** with native multimodal reasoning. Gemini 3.5 Flash is now the default model powering Google Search. ([ToolsCompare AI](https://toolscompare.ai/news/june-2026))

- **Nemotron 3 Ultra 550B (NVIDIA)** — A 550B parameter (55B active) open-weights model released June 4, described as "the most intelligent US open-weights model." ([LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news))

- **MiniMax M3** — First open-weight model combining frontier-tier software engineering with a **1-million-token context window** and native multimodal computer use. Reduces per-token compute to **1/20th** of previous models and achieves 9× faster prefilling. ([AIapps](https://www.aiapps.com/blog/ai-news-breakthroughs-launches-trends-must-read/))

- **MAI-Code-1-Flash & MAI-Thinking-1 (Microsoft)** — Released June 2 at Build; Microsoft's first in-house coding and reasoning models designed to reduce developer reliance on OpenAI. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/microsoft-unveils-new-ai-models-lessen-reliance-on-openai-lower-costs.html))

- **OpenRouter Fusion** — A compound model API claiming "Fable-level intelligence at half the price," gaining rapid adoption as a Fable 5 substitute. ([AI Coding Daily](https://aicodingdaily.substack.com/p/kimi-k27-code-fable-5-ban-and-cursor))

- **ZoomMate (Zoom)** — Launched June 1 at **$20/user/month**. Integrates directly into live meetings, transforms notes into polished documents, and connects with Salesforce, Jira, and ServiceNow. ([AIapps](https://www.aiapps.com/blog/ai-news-breakthroughs-launches-trends-must-read/))

## Breakthroughs & Research

- **CVPR 2026 Best Papers** were announced June 5-6, recognising the year's most innovative computer vision and AI research. ([CVPR](https://cvpr.thecvf.com/Conferences/2026/News/Best_Papers) / [Newswise](https://www.newswise.com/articles/cvpr-2026-honors-the-year-s-most-innovative-computer-vision-and-ai-research))

- **TurboQuant (Google, ICLR 2026)** — An algorithm that significantly reduces the memory overhead of the KV cache using PolarQuant vector rotation and Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss compression, enabling larger context windows at lower memory cost. ([Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates))

- **Mollifier Layers (University of Pennsylvania)** — A technique integrating classical mathematical smoothing functions into neural networks to solve inverse partial differential equations with greater stability. Applications span genomics, materials science, climate modeling, and chromatin biology. ([Build This Now](https://www.buildthisnow.com/blog/guide/mechanics/ai-research-june-2026))

- **Climate AI (UC San Diego)** — Generative methods combined with physics-based data enable climate models to run **25× faster** than traditional approaches, enabling much more granular scenario planning. ([Build This Now](https://www.buildthisnow.com/blog/guide/mechanics/ai-research-june-2026))

- **Orion-100B** — A 100-billion-parameter model was trained for just **$1.25/hour** using commodity hardware, compared to the typical $50/hour datacenter cost, achieving 65% of traditional training speeds. A significant cost-efficiency milestone. ([AIapps](https://www.aiapps.com/blog/ai-news-breakthroughs-launches-trends-must-read/))

- **OpenAI Math Breakthrough** — An OpenAI model successfully disproved a central conjecture in discrete geometry, marking a milestone for AI in advanced mathematical research. ([ToolsCompare AI](https://toolscompare.ai/news/june-2026))

- **MycoBCP** — A deep learning system helping researchers detect subtle tuberculosis cell changes, accelerating treatment discovery. ([Build This Now](https://www.buildthisnow.com/blog/guide/mechanics/ai-research-june-2026))

- **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** adoption grew **58% quarter-over-quarter** in Q2 2026, transitioning from a niche developer standard to a foundational layer across major agent frameworks. ([AIapps](https://www.aiapps.com/blog/ai-news-breakthroughs-launches-trends-must-read/))
