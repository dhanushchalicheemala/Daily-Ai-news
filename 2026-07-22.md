## New Models & Tools

- **Google released Gemini 3.6 Flash, 3.5 Flash-Lite, and 3.5 Flash Cyber** on July 21. Gemini 3.6 Flash is pitched as Google's new "workhorse" model, using up to 17% fewer output tokens than 3.5 Flash while taking fewer reasoning steps and tool calls for multi-step agentic workflows; it's priced at $1.50/M input and $7.50/M output tokens (down from $9/M output). Flash-Lite targets low-cost use cases, and Flash Cyber is a specialized model fine-tuned for finding and fixing cybersecurity vulnerabilities, initially limited to a government/trusted-partner pilot. Google also teased an upcoming Gemini 4. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/21/google-releases-three-new-gemini-models-but-no-3-5-pro/)
- **Gemini 3.6 Flash is now available in GitHub Copilot**, rolling out the same day as its general release. [GitHub Changelog](https://github.blog/changelog/2026-07-21-gemini-3-6-flash-is-now-available-in-github-copilot/)

## Breakthroughs & Research

- Coverage of **ICML 2026** continues to highlight "selective activation sparsity," a training technique that lets models match the performance of models roughly 3x their size by activating only task-relevant parameters — part of a broader industry push toward cheaper, more efficient inference amid the ongoing model price war. [Skycrumbs](https://skycrumbs.com/blog/ai-research-july-2026)

## Company & Product Updates

- **Google's Flash-tier price cuts add pressure to the ongoing AI inference price war**, following Grok 4.5, GPT-5.6, and Meta's Muse Spark 1.1 all shipping within the same week in early July — the industry narrative has shifted from "best model wins" to "best fit wins," with price, speed, and access weighing as heavily as raw benchmark scores. [Cryptobriefing](https://cryptobriefing.com/google-launches-gemini-3-6-flash-alongside-flash-lite-and-cyber-models/)
