## New Models & Tools

- **Three frontier model families go generally available on the same day**: OpenAI's GPT-5.6 (Sol, Terra, and Luna tiers), xAI's Grok 4.5, and Meta Superintelligence Labs' Muse Spark 1.1 all launched broadly on July 9 — the first time three frontier-class model releases have landed simultaneously. GPT-5.6 Sol is priced at $5/$30 per million input/output tokens, Terra at $2.50/$15, and Luna at $1/$6; Grok 4.5 API runs $2/$6. [Source](https://releasebot.io/updates/openai)
- **OpenAI launches ChatGPT Work**, a GPT-5.6-powered agent that pulls context from apps, files, and workflows to autonomously produce finished documents, spreadsheets, presentations, and websites, running continuously for hours on complex projects. It rolls out first to Pro, Enterprise, and Edu users, with a new unified ChatGPT desktop app merging Chat, Work, and Codex across all plans, including Free. [Source](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-09/openai-unveils-chatgpt-work-agent-to-field-tasks-for-hours)
- **Meta's Muse Image and Muse Video** launched as companion generative media tools alongside the Muse Spark 1.1 model, which offers a 1M-token context window, multimodal input, and computer-use capability for developers. [Source](https://www.engadget.com/2211869/openai-releases-chatgpt-work-tool-macos-windows-web-plans/)
- The developer API market now has five actively accessible frontier-class tiers — Fable 5, GPT-5.6 Sol, Grok 4.5, Sonnet 5, and Muse Spark 1.1 — sharpening price and capability competition heading into the second half of 2026. [Source](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-9-2026)

## Breakthroughs & Research

- **Google's Gemini for Science** builds on two Nature-published research systems — Empirical Research Assistance (ERA), which helps scientists write expert-level empirical software, and Co-Scientist — accelerating discoveries spanning neuroscience to cosmology. [Source](https://research.google/blog/a-new-era-of-innovation-google-research-at-io-2026/)
- **Ames Laboratory's DuctGPT**, a physics-trained AI workflow, is being used to discover rare-earth-free permanent magnets, a materials-science advance with implications for motors and clean-energy hardware. [Source](https://blog.mean.ceo/latest-ai-breakthroughs-news-july-2026/)
- A new **AI-powered framework for measuring cosmic expansion** analyzes images of Type Ia supernovae and their host environments in unprecedented detail, offering astronomers a faster route to refining estimates of the universe's expansion rate. [Source](https://blog.mean.ceo/latest-ai-breakthroughs-news-july-2026/)
